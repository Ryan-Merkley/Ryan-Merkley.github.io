Scripted Narrative: A Day in the Life

Roles: Cinematographer, Organizer

Summary: A short, fictional narrative film that a team of students and I shot and edited. A Day in the Life is a psychological thriller that follows the main protagonist Dan while he goes through his daily routines.

Tools Used: Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe After Effects, Google Drive (Docs and Sheets), Canon 70D, Shotgun Microphone

Created by: Ryan Merkley, Tabinda Ahmed, Danielle Park, Minahil Sheikh, Brianna Coens

_____

The Challenge

This film was created in correspondence to my digital media course. The challenge for this group project was being able to produce a short, fictional narrative film in a small team and with a limited budget.

The Process

Throughout the initial stages of this project, there were many core aspects of production that we had to fulfill before we began filming. Once we had brainstormed ideas and chosen a plot for our film, we then had to write a script, scout for locations, and hire actors all within a small budget. We were very fortunate to hire two very skilled actors from our university who were willing to act for free and have a group member who volunteered their house for the filming location. During the filming days, we would arrive early to set up the equipment and film multiple scenes on the same day as a convenience factor for our actor and actress.

After completion of the filming process, we then began the editing process. We encountered a major issue during the final stages of the editing process. A couple of days before the project was due, all our files had become corrupted. Immediately after receiving this information, we quickly initiated a group meeting to assess all the necessary information about the situation. After realizing that we were going to have to restart editing, we informed our professor and she kindly extended the project deadline. For the next few days, we set designated times for group meetings so that we could help each other with their parts as well as provide backups for everyoneâ€™s work. We successfully completed our project a couple days later, presented it to our class, and obtained a mark of 90%.

My Contributions

I was the cinematographer for this film and was responsible for the visualization of our story and filming. Once our team had finalized the script, I created a storyboard and decided to go to the filming location to visualize the shots ahead of time. I documented various aspects of the film throughout the storyboard such as lighting, camera angles and prop placements. On the filming days, I would show up early to set up the equipment and make sure everything was ready to go before our actors arrived. While filming, I ensured that the camera was properly placed, the camera settings were adjusted, and the Director and Sound Crew were ready for each scene.

Skills Learned

Skills that I developed throughout this project were my team working and communication skills. During the designing stage, I collaborated with the Director in developing the vision for the film by showing potential scenes and having my storyboard reviewed. I would actively communicate with each team member during our filming days to confirm that every stage of the production was set up and that everyone understood their roles. I also assisted those who were struggling to set up their equipment to guarantee successful production of each scene as well as made sure our actor and actress were comfortable by offering them food and drinks.
