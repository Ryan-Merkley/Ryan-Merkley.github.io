FishToday

Roles: Designer, Usability Tester

Summary: A multimedia project focusing on resolving the lack of support for the fishing community.

Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Google Drive (Docs and Sheets), InVision, Balsamiq Mockups

Created by: Ryan Merkley, Arturo Salek, Mathieu Legault, Matthew Southgate


Prototype App

_____

The Challenge

The challenge for this group project was to develop a concept for a digital product by using user centered design. The goal was to take an idea from a problem, conduct user experience research and user testing on that idea, and then eventually create an interactive prototype to solve that problem.

The Process

For this project we were given the concept of sports but, after some brainstorming we realized that most sports already had their own individual websites, expect for fishing. Upon obtaining this information, we decided that we would create a website and app solely around fishing that would allow local fishermen to find fishing buddies, fishing gear, and plan for fishing trips all in one convenient application. Once we had identified our problem, we then designed user personas for each type of user that would be using our solution. These personas gave us insight into the motivations and preferences of each user and helped guide the designs for our wireframes.

After completion of our wireframes, we then decided to conduct user testing on each section so that we could discover key problem areas and improve upon those areas to make them easier to use. The feedback that we acquired from this testing was critical to the completed version of the prototype. We learned about many areas of our app that users found confusing and this gave us the opportunity to adjust our final design in a way that truly supported the user‑friendly experience.

My Contributions

I was responsible for the design of the Find Spots section of our app. I used Adobe Illustrator to design this section and incorporated the use of a map to show available finishing locations. While designing this section, I frequently used guerrilla testing to obtain feedback on my designs. I went to local fishing stores and had actual fishermen test the mockup version of this section. They were able to provide me with information regarding features the weren't necessary and features that I could improve upon to make the app more user‑friendly.

Skills Learned

A skill that I believe allowed me to succeed in this project was my ability to accept and learn from criticism. I found that feedback from my designs was helpful in enhancing the quality of my work. It forced me to think about my designs in a way where everyone could benefit not just myself.
