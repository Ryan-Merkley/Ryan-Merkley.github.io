Website Brand Development: Plugged

Roles: Designer, Project Manager

Summary: A group of students and I designed a prototype website for an intermediary music business called Plugged who institute connections between venues, artists, and audiences.

Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe XD, Google Drive (Docs and Sheets), Balsamiq Mockups

Created by: Ryan Merkley, Adrien Vilcini, Wyatt Kay, Yavuz Temeltas, James Colalillo, Alek Policicchio


Prototype

_____

The Challenge

The challenge that my team was required to do was to design a website that would help develop a brand for a business. For this project, we were assigned to a business called Plugged. Plugged is the intermediary between venues, artists, and audiences. They initiate communications between the involved parties, create a community with common interests and promote new music.

The Process

Our objective when creating this website was trying to connect Plugged's three primary customer groups; the artist, the audience, and the venue. We wanted the pages for each customer group to be unique, while still maintaining connections with one another. We began this project by conducting brainstorming sessions to determine how might we make this connection possible. During these sessions we would go over possible designs and layouts as well as individual research from each team member. These discussions led to the first iteration of our prototype.

We then ran into a major problem of our client disliking all our designs. Once we had presented our pitch, our client immediately let us know about their displeasure and how our designs didn’t accurately reflect their company. We didn’t let this feedback discourage us and instead we acknowledged our client’s disappointment and went back into brainstorming to determine what went wrong. After critically analyzing our designs, we noticed that we had focused too much on the literal meaning of the company. We decided to break down the company’s description into smaller pieces and this helped design a website that was more in line with the company’s overall goals. Through this process, we made sure to continually communicate with our client by showing them our ideas and rough work. This ultimately helped resolve the problem and led to a completed prototype that our team and client was proud of.

My Contributions

For this project, I contributed to the design of the company logo and the Discover page. For my design of the logo, I first conducted research about our client’s main goals and used this information to design the logo. I wanted the logo to connect to their target market and therefore created it featuring their three customer groups. The logo is composed of a location pin for the venue, a record for the artist, and headphones for the audience. Once we had finished the designs for the logo, I then created a mock-up design of the Discover page to present to our client. When creating the final design, I incorporated a search menu on top of the mapping display to allow users to search for events and click on them to learn more about that venue.

Skills Learned

A skill that I used effectively in this project was my leadership skills. Being the project manager, I made sure to initiate meetings with my team to discuss project ideas as well as with our client to broadcast our designs. When we ran into the problem of our client disliking our ideas, I ensured that my team stayed calm and didn’t overreact to our client’s concerns. I frequently communicated with our client through emails to keep them up to date with our work. Finally, I made sure that everyone in our group had a part to work on for the project and regularly checked up on them to make sure that they weren’t struggling and if they needed any assistance with their work.
