Animated Interview: The Coat Hanger Experience

Roles: Animator, Video Editor

Summary: An animation of a pre-recorded interview about a woman’s experience as a child with a coat hanger.

Tools Used: Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe After Effects, Adobe Illustrator, Zoom H4n Pro

Created by: Ryan Merkley, Daniel Recchia, Matthew Southgate, Minku Han, Eric Lamoureux

_____

The Challenge

The challenge for this group project revolved around visually storytelling a pre-recorded interview. The focus was on effectively utilizing sound recording and editing, animation, character development, and creative problem solving.

The Process

When we were presented with this project, our team decided to conduct as many interviews as possible by going around our university campus and recording different individuals. This allowed us to acquire a variety of recordings all with different potential. Our team chose the recording of a woman's experience as a child with a coat hanger because of the ability to incorporate a variety of themes throughout the video as well as having the creative freedom with each scene. We then had a brainstorming session to determine what the characters and environment should look like. This was important because we needed to make sure that there was consistency among the designs. After coming to a consensus for the designs, we began our individual sections of the animation. To ensure that we maintained consistency, we would have weekly team meetings to critique and go over everyone’s designs.

After completing the animation, we then scheduled a feedback session. During this session we had multiple students watch and review our animation. This was helpful as it gave us insight into some overlooked issues such as the sound quality being low, the video lagging and the background environment shaking. After fixing these errors, we presented our final animation to our class where we received a positive response from both our professor and classmates as well as obtaining a grade of 93%.

My Contributions

I was responsible for animating the scene when the hanger gets caught in the sister’s eye. While animating this scene, I encountered a significant problem with my designs. When I initially designed each character, I created them as a single design, but when I started animating, I found out that the entire body was moving. To fix this, I re-designed the characters body parts as individual pieces, and this allowed me to animate each limb separately to make them move. Upon the completion of everyone’s sections, I was also responsible for finalizing the animation. I used Adobe Premiere Pro to join the scenes together and match the audio with the lip‑syncing scenes.

Skills Learned

Skills that I found I was able to effectively use throughout this project include my communication and leadership skills. Every week, I would make sure to contact each of my group members to check in with them to see how they were doing and if they needed any help. I would also conduct weekly team meetings to allow everyone to show their progress and to determine whether our designs were following a consistent pattern. Finally, I regularly communicated with my group over Facebook Messenger and email, sending them pictures and short video clips of my designs to obtain feedback before going any further into the animation.
